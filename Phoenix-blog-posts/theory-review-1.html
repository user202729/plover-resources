It’s the first Monday of January and time to start on our theory review!

Theory Review 1 will cover outlines that are influenced by the spelling of the word.

While Phoenix Theory is primarily a phonetic theory, there are a few instances when we must pay attention to the spelling of the English word to determine the correct outline for the word. The purpose? To resolve conflicts and to create consistency in responses. Let’s review those today:

Words spelled w-a-r (that sound similar to “or”) are stroked WAR: war /WAR, warble /WARBL, ward /WARD, warm /WARM, warn /WARN, warp /WARP, warrant /WARNT, wart /WART.

Single-syllable words spelled -i-g-h-t that sound like “ite” are stroked OIGT: blight /BLOIGT, bright /BROIGT, fight /FOIGT, flight /FLOIGT, fright /FROIGT, height /HOIGT, knight /KPWOIGT, light /LOIGT, might /MOIGT, night /NOIGT, plight /PLOIGT, right /ROIGT, sight /SOIGT, slight /SLOIGT, tight /TOIGT. This frees up the phonetic Long I outline to create compound words: airtight /AIR/TAOIT, backlight /BAK/LAOIT, birthright /BURGT/RAOIT, etc.

Words beginning with a kn- spelling are stroked KPW-: knap /KPWAP, knave /KPWAIV, knead /KPWAOED, knee /KPWAOE, kneel /KPWAOEL, knew /KPWAOU, knight /KPWOIGT, knit /KPWEUT, knot /KPWAUT, know /KPWOE, etc.

Words beginning with a qu- spelling are stroked KW-: queen /KWAOEN, quit /KWEUT, queue /KWAOU, etc.

Words beginning with a silent H have the H included in the outline: hour /HOUR, honor /HAUN/-R or /HAURN, etc.

Words beginning with a wh- spelling are stroked WH-: wheat /WHAOET, wheel /WHAOEL, whet /WHET, Whig /WHIG, while /WHAOIL, whine /WHAOIN, etc.

Words beginning with a wr- spelling are stroked WR-: wrack /WRAK, wrap /WRAP, wretch /WREFP, wring /WRING, write /WRAOIT, wrote /WROET, wry WRAOI, etc.

Single-syllable words ending in -mb are stroked -M/-B: bomb /BAUM/-B, climb /KLAOIM/-B, comb /KOEM/-B, crumb /KRUM/-B, jamb /JAM/-B, lamb /LAM/-B, limb /LIM/-B, numb /NUM/-B, plumb /PLUM/-B, thumb /THUM/-B, tomb /TAOUM/-B, womb /WAOUM/-B.

If you want one-stroke alternatives for these, define and write as follows: bomb /BA*UM, climb /KLAO*IM, comb /KO*EM, crumb /KR*UM, jamb /JA*M, lamb /LA*M, limb /L*IM (will overwrite an entry), numb /N*UM, plumb /PL*UM, thumb /TH*UM, tomb /TAO*UM, womb WAO*UM.

Single-stroke words ending in -ss are stroked -SZ: ass /ASZ, bass /BASZ, bless /BLESZ, bliss /BLISZ, etc.

Single-stroke words spelled oo are stroked AO: cook /KAOK, took /TAOK, tooth /TAOGT, soon /SAON, boot /BAOT, booze /BAOZ, etc.
